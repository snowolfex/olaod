# Plush Llama App Art Directions

## Goal

Use the plush llama as a soft branded companion across Oload surfaces without letting it overwhelm the product UI.

## Shared character rules

- always plush, soft, and premium
- always non-pink
- keep the face readable at small sizes
- avoid complex poses when the character is secondary to UI content
- use the same core palette across all surfaces

## Splash screen

- use a larger bust or seated pose
- let the llama carry more personality here
- slight head tilt is fine
- background can include soft studio shapes, clouds, or abstract warm forms
- keep the scene calm, not hyper-busy

## Help / onboarding

- use the llama as a guide character
- pair the mascot with gesture-like poses: looking, pointing, listening, waving
- keep expressions reassuring and clear
- use simplified body language so it reads fast beside text

## PDF manual art

- keep the llama simpler than splash art
- favor bust, half-body, or seated profile moments
- use the mascot as a section anchor rather than a full scene subject
- keep line-of-sight and pose aimed toward text blocks or diagrams

## Empty states

- use the most minimal version here
- one plush bust or sitting pose
- very light expression
- no heavy accessories or elaborate backgrounds

## App icon / badge studies

- head-only or bust only
- centered silhouette
- fluffy forehead tuft and ears must stay visible
- eye spacing and nose placement must remain readable at small scale
- do not rely on thin seam details for recognition

## Accessory guidance

- good: tiny scarf, small stitched collar, subtle badge, soft bow
- avoid: hats that hide the ears, large props, noisy costume pieces

## Best first implementation order

1. head-only icon study
2. help/onboarding bust illustration
3. splash-art full mascot scene
4. PDF section mascots

## Tone to preserve

- friendly
- local-first
- safe
- calm
- premium
- softly playful
